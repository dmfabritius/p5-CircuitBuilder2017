Things to be aware of when attempting to use the circuit sketching tool:

  • It's not perfect
  • The main user interface is the pop-up menu
    • Press and hold the mouse button to interact with the menu
    • The menu is accessed using either the left or right mouse buttons
    • The left button is probably easier because the brower's context menu is not blocked
    • Release when hovering over the desired option to make that happen
    • Release anywhere else to simply close the menu
    • When the mouse is off the canvas, pressing the button does nothing
    • When the mouse is on the canvas but off the circuit, pressing the button shows circuit-level options
    • When the mouse is on the circuit over a blank spot, you can add a component
    • When the mouse is over a component, you can modify it
    • Some components cover more than one spot, but only their top-left spot is interactive
    • Rotating or flipping a component does not change its interactive spot (but does move it)
  • Click on a spot in the circuit to select it (be careful not to accidently select a menu option)
  • Hold shift and click another spot to select an area
  • Hold control and click multiple spots to select them
  • Holding shift or control when selecting a menu action will effect all the selected spots/components
  • Ctrl-C, X, & V will copy, cut, and paste the selection
  • Ctrl-Z will undo up to 100 steps -- there is no re-do, so be careful
  • Press the arrow keys to move around all the selected components
  • Hold shift and press the arrow keys to move the circuit on the canvas
  • Press the home key to reposition the circuit to the top-left corner of the canvas
  • Hold the shift key and use the scroll wheel to rotate all selected components individually
  • Use the mouse scroll wheel by itself to zoom in and out
  • Different browsers may also react to the mouse wheel if the window has scroll bars
  • After saving to JSON, you can use a text editor to modify the circuit's width, height, etc.
    • When you open a circuit with a modified size, the component positions will be scambled
    • You can fix it by clearing the circuit (keeping its size) and then merging it back in again
